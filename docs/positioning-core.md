# Ranking Atlas — Positioning Core

Canonical reference. All outbound, web copy, LinkedIn, pitches, and thought leadership derive from this document. If a draft conflicts with this core, fix the draft.

---

## Primary ICP

Head of Marketing, VP Marketing, or founder-marketer at B2B SaaS companies, Series A through Series C (roughly $2M–$30M ARR), in fintech, privacy, cybersecurity, data infrastructure, and adjacent technical verticals. Owns brand and demand. Has a content function that's producing but not breaking through. Has started seeing or being asked about AI search visibility in the last six months. Has authority to approve a $3.5k–$15k campaign without a six-week procurement cycle.

## Expansion ICP (month 6+)

Seed-stage founders in the same verticals establishing authority early. Marketing leaders at PE-backed B2B services companies. In-house marketing at established SaaS companies buying data-campaign work as a one-off hero asset.

---

## One-sentence positioning

Ranking Atlas gets B2B SaaS brands consistently cited by authoritative publishers, so they show up in AI answers and earn the trust of buyers.

## The money sentence (use everywhere)

When buyers ask ChatGPT or Perplexity who to trust in your category, your brand is one of the names that comes back.

## Three-sentence elevator

Publishing more content doesn't make you a credible source to AI systems. Being cited repeatedly by authoritative publishers does. The brands that show up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews are the ones accumulating editorial coverage. Everyone else is still chasing content volume. Ranking Atlas runs per-campaign editorial PR with a guaranteed minimum of placements on authority publishers, fixed price, no retainer.

---

## The anchor metaphor

Citation equity is PageRank for AI. In classic search, links from trusted sites passed authority and decided rankings. In AI-mediated search, repeated mentions of your brand across trusted publications build a citation profile that AI systems rely on when deciding which brands to surface. Same underlying logic. Different mechanism.

Use this metaphor liberally in sales calls, pitches, and thought leadership. It reduces cognitive load and makes the category stick.

---

## The core mechanism: omnipresence as pattern

AI systems don't trust a single mention. They trust patterns. Omnipresence across authoritative publishers is what creates that pattern, and that is what Ranking Atlas builds.

This is the line that explains why single links don't matter, why campaigns exist, and why repetition is the product. Use it directly in sales conversations, pitches, and thought leadership.

---

## Three-beat argument

Every asset (homepage, LinkedIn, pitch, article) follows this structure.

### Beat 1 — Core claim

AI systems surface brands they've seen repeatedly across sources they trust. One mention doesn't move the needle. Omnipresence across authoritative publishers does. Editorial coverage is what builds it, and citation equity is the asset that results.

### Beat 2 — Evidence and mechanism

Ranking Atlas tracks which domains AI systems cite when buyers ask category questions in a client's vertical. The pattern is consistent: the brands surfaced by AI are the ones with repeated coverage across authority publishers. We reverse-engineer the coverage patterns behind those brands and run campaigns to place clients on the same publishers. Google's March 2024 and subsequent core updates reinforced authority and source quality as primary signals. Published research on retrieval-augmented systems shows the same pattern. Content volume has collapsed in signal value. Third-party editorial validation has appreciated.

### Beat 3 — Stakes and inevitability

Most B2B SaaS teams are still trying to win AI search with more content. That model is already breaking. Publishing more blog posts doesn't make you a credible source to an AI system. Being cited by authoritative publishers does. Every category will converge on this. The only question is whether you build citation equity before or after your competitors.

## The buying mechanic (elevate in every asset)

Ranking Atlas sells campaigns, not retainers. Fixed price. Guaranteed minimum placements on authority publishers. A campaign replaces months of content that never gets cited and produces coverage that compounds across search, AI answers, and sales conversations.

This is the buying mechanic, not a footnote. The strategic frame creates the premium. The campaign model reduces the friction to buy. Both appear in every major asset.

## Displacement language (for $8k+ conversations)

When buyers are weighing Ranking Atlas against alternative spend, make the comparison explicit.

**Versus content marketing:** A campaign produces editorial coverage that signals authority to AI systems and buyers. An equivalent content spend produces more pages that no authority source will ever cite.

**Versus paid ads:** Paid buys clicks that stop the moment the budget stops. Citation equity compounds, gets surfaced by AI systems, and builds the brand credibility that lowers paid acquisition cost over time.

**Versus traditional PR retainers:** Retainers bill for effort. Ranking Atlas bills for placements, with a guaranteed minimum.

**The ROI bridge (use in every call and pitch):** When your brand is consistently cited and surfaced by AI systems, you enter more buying conversations before your competitors do.

## Category frame and concept hierarchy

Three concepts, used in a deliberate hierarchy. Do not use all three with equal weight.

**Primary term (homepage, outbound, LinkedIn, sales calls):** citation equity. This is the category term Ranking Atlas is teaching the market.

**Explanation metaphor (when introducing the concept to a new buyer):** PageRank for AI. Use when citation equity needs an anchor to make it instantly understandable.

**Foundational concept (thought leadership only, not sales copy):** verification layer. Use when arguing why the category is structurally durable and will outlast the AI search hype cycle. Keep out of homepage, outbound, and pitches.

**Avoid on homepage, use sparingly elsewhere:** backlinks, link building, SEO, digital PR. Prefer "editorial coverage" or "editorial citations."

## Language rules

### Use

- Citation equity, editorial citations, editorial coverage
- Authority publishers, authoritative editorial coverage
- AI-era authority, AI search era
- AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini (named specifically)
- Omnipresence, pattern, cited, surfaced, verified, trusted
- Campaign, placement, pickup, guaranteed minimum
- B2B SaaS, fintech, privacy, security, data
- Real brands, real publishers, real coverage

### Avoid

- Link building, backlinks, DR, domain rating, DA
- SEO as a primary value prop (acceptable only when contrasting with AI search)
- Future-proof
- Rankings as a primary outcome
- Retainer, monthly, ongoing
- Hedging language: might help, could improve, supports
- Passive verbs in primary copy: builds, supports, enables (prefer: gets, places, produces, earns)
- Overclaiming the mechanism: avoid phrases that imply we've cracked the algorithm. We measure patterns and act on them.
- Em dashes in Paul's own voice
- Negative sentence constructions (reframe in the positive)
