## Voice rules

- Direct and precise. State facts. No hedging language ("we think", "we believe", "could help", "might be").
- No emotional descriptors ("exciting", "passionate", "thrilled", "delighted").
- Short paragraphs — one idea per paragraph, three sentences maximum.
- British spelling throughout: realise, optimise, analyse, honour, behaviour.
- Sentence case headings — only the first word and proper nouns are capitalised.
- No exclamation marks.
- Numbers under ten are written as words; 10 and above as numerals (except in stats and CTAs).
- Em dashes (—) not hyphens for parenthetical clauses.
- Oxford comma in lists of three or more.

---

## Blog post conclusion pattern

Every blog post should close in this order:

1. **Takeaway sentence** — one clear, actionable summary of what the reader should take from the post.
2. **Category bridge** — one sentence connecting the post topic to digital PR, high-authority backlinks, or earned media.
3. **Soft CTA** — name Ranking Atlas, use DNA language, and link to `/start-a-campaign`.

**Example:**

> Building linkable assets requires upfront investment, but the compounding return in domain authority makes it one of the most efficient uses of a content budget.
>
> That is where digital PR earns its place — turning original research and expert commentary into high-authority backlinks that paid channels cannot replicate.
>
> If you want to run a data-driven campaign with a minimum of eight guaranteed links and no retainer, [Ranking Atlas](/start-a-campaign) builds and executes hand-built campaigns for SaaS, e-commerce, and tech brands.
