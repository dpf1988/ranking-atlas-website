Methodology & sources

Data sources: The Aesthetic Society (ASAPS) Cosmetic Surgery National Data Bank (years 2001–2017, 2019) and the American Society of Plastic Surgeons (ASPS) Plastic Surgery Statistics Report (years 2018, 2020, 2022, 2023, 2024). Both organizations publish national age-bracket counts per procedure; mean age is computed by Ranking Atlas as a count-weighted mean of bracket midpoints.

Coverage: 24 consecutive years of real measured data, 2001–2024. The single exception is 2021, for which neither ASAPS nor ASPS published an annual report; that year is linearly interpolated between 2020 and 2022 ASPS values and shown with an open marker.

Methodology shift (2018–2019): ASAPS narrowed its respondent pool from a multi-specialty sample (plastic surgeons, dermatologists, otolaryngologists) to plastic surgeons only beginning with its 2019 report. ASPS uses a different bracket structure (13–19, 20–29, 30–39, 40–54, 55+) than ASAPS pre-2019 (18-and-under, 19–34, 35–50, 51–64, 65+). The orange band on the chart marks this transition; cross-source comparisons inside that window are sensitive to ~1–3 years of bracket-boundary drift.

Year not yet available: The ASPS 2025 report is expected mid-2026; 2024 is the most recent year with published data as of April 2026.
